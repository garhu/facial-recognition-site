Arrested For
Someone Else's Face

Dangerously flawed facial recognition systems are becoming alarmingly pervasive within law enforcement.

By Gary Hu

December 8, 2021

On a Thursday afternoon in January 2020, Robert Julian-Borchak Williams drove home from work to his home in Farmington Hills, Michigan. When he pulled into his driveway, he was subsequently arrested on his lawn in front of his wife and two daughters. Police refused to disclose what he was being arrested for. Instead, he was only shown a piece of paper with his photo and the words “felony warrant” and “larceny.”
